# Offer Funnel Scoring Rubric

Score each of the ten dimensions from 0 to 10. Use the anchors. Do not invent half-points without a reason. A funnel below 60/100 is leaking money; below 40/100 it is preventing qualified inquiries.

Output format per dimension:

```
<dimension>: <score>/10
  Evidence: <route/file/section + quote>
  Gap: <what is missing or wrong>
  Move: <one concrete change>
```

---

## 1. Buyer Fit (0–10)

- 0–2: Page targets "everyone" or "businesses". No industry, no role, no project size.
- 3–5: Industry hinted (Solar/SHK) but role and deal size unclear. Reads like a portfolio page.
- 6–8: Clear industry, role (Geschäftsführer/Vertriebsleiter), and project-value floor. Poor-fit visitors can self-exclude.
- 9–10: A wrong-fit visitor would close the tab within 5 seconds and a right-fit visitor would feel "this is for me" in the hero alone.

Killer signals (auto -3): "Webdesign", "Online-Marketing", "WordPress-Agentur" in hero/CTA on cold acquisition routes.

## 2. Pain Economics (0–10)

- 0–2: Pain is abstract ("mehr Anfragen", "besser sichtbar").
- 3–5: Pain named (Portal-Abhängigkeit, Leadkosten) but no numbers, no consequence.
- 6–8: Concrete CPL/Portalanteil/Vertriebsstunden + cost-of-doing-nothing.
- 9–10: Buyer can calculate their own monthly loss within seconds of reading.

Killer signal (auto -3): inflated numbers without source or constraint.

## 3. Offer Ladder (0–10)

- 0–2: Single "Kontakt" CTA. No ladder.
- 3–5: Ladder exists but steps are not differentiated (Marktcheck and Anfrage feel identical).
- 6–8: Marktcheck → Analyse → Umsetzung → Retainer is visible, with distinct jobs.
- 9–10: Each step states its decision criterion, what the buyer learns, and what happens next.

Killer signal (auto -3): Umsetzung pitched before Analyse on a cold route.

## 4. Marketcheck Desire (0–10)

- 0–2: "Kostenlose Analyse anfordern" — generic, low perceived value.
- 3–5: Some value framed (you receive X) but no proof of rigor.
- 6–8: Buyer understands the deliverable, the rigor, and what they avoid by doing it.
- 9–10: Buyer feels the Marktcheck is more valuable than competitor's paid audit and worth submitting real business context.

Killer signal (auto -3): "in 48 Stunden", "kostenlos" framed as the main benefit instead of decision value.

## 5. Proof Architecture (0–10)

- 0–2: No named proof. Generic testimonials or none.
- 3–5: E3 mentioned but used as a guarantee, not a mechanism.
- 6–8: E3 with before/after, mechanism, constraints, transferability.
- 9–10: Proof placed immediately before every major ask. Each claim is bounded by the constraint that makes it credible.

Killer signal (auto -3): E3 generalized to all industries/budgets without constraint.

## 6. Qualification Design (0–10)

- 0–2: Form asks name/email/message only.
- 3–5: Asks company and project but not the qualifying economics.
- 6–8: Asks Industry, Projektvolumen, aktuelle Leadquelle, CPL, Portalanteil — enough to triage before a call.
- 9–10: Form actively disqualifies wrong-fit and converts the act of filling into a self-diagnosis.

Killer signal (auto -3): Single open-text "Ihre Nachricht" with no fit fields.

## 7. CTA Economics (0–10)

- 0–2: CTAs say "Kontakt" or "Mehr erfahren".
- 3–5: CTAs name the action but not the value.
- 6–8: CTAs name the value received and the next step.
- 9–10: Primary CTA is identical at every decision point. Secondaries support, never compete.

Killer signal (auto -3): More than one primary CTA visually competing in the same viewport.

## 8. WGOS Boundary (0–10)

- 0–2: WGOS in hero, primary CTA, or marketcheck entry copy on Solar/SHK route.
- 3–5: WGOS named publicly but on a delivery/methodology route.
- 6–8: WGOS only in proposal, delivery, post-sale, or protected/noindex detail.
- 9–10: Cold visitor never has to learn "WGOS" to understand the business outcome. WGOS is reserved as delivery architecture and proposal language.

Killer signal (auto -5): "WordPress Growth Operating System" in hero copy on a Solar/SHK acquisition route.

## 9. Sales Handoff (0–10)

- 0–2: No timeline, price range, or risk addressed.
- 3–5: Some objections covered in an FAQ at the bottom.
- 6–8: Timing, fit, price range, ownership, risk addressed before the final CTA.
- 9–10: The lead arrives at sales already pre-qualified, with expectations matching the offer.

Killer signal (auto -3): FAQ that creates objections instead of resolving them.

## 10. Operational Truth (0–10)

- 0–2: Promises sales/delivery cannot keep. Outdated timing. Old Shopify/Growth-Audit language.
- 3–5: A few legacy claims linger.
- 6–8: Copy matches what is actually delivered. Outdated framings retired.
- 9–10: Every claim is one the team can defend in a sales call without softening.

Killer signal (auto -5): "48 Stunden" promise that delivery cannot reliably keep.

---

## Total

- 90–100: Closeable demand machine. Optimize CPL.
- 75–89: Strong. Fix P1s.
- 60–74: Leaking. Fix P0s before paid traffic.
- 40–59: Costing money. Stop paid traffic, fix ladder + qualification + WGOS boundary.
- < 40: Acquisition surface is anti-helpful. Rebuild ladder before any further copy work.

A world-class score requires honest numbers from the buyer's perspective, not the operator's.
